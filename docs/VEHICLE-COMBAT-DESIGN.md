## 1. Setting: Painted Modern Earth

### The Premise

You are a **Particle Life blob** -- a self-organizing particle entity that escaped a classified CSIRO research facility in Far North Queensland. Humans are the enemy. The ADF classified you as a containment breach before anyone realised you were alive.

Earth is rendered through the existing NPR watercolor pipeline:
- **Kuwahara filter** transforms hard urban geometry into soft, painterly strokes
- **Lab-space color transfer** gives concrete and asphalt a warm, illustrated palette
- **Ink edge detection** outlines buildings, cars, and humans like panel borders in a graphic novel
- **Paper grain texture** makes the entire world look printed on watercolor stock

### The World

Modern Earth environments, all procedurally constructed from Three.js primitives:

- **Suburban streets**: Rows of box-geometry houses, lamp posts, parked cars, fire hydrants
- **Parking lots**: Grid layouts with scattered vehicles to hijack
- **Strip malls and gas stations**: Simple building shells with signage planes
- **Downtown blocks**: Taller box buildings, alleys, intersections with traffic lights
- **Industrial zones**: Warehouses, scrap yards (component caches), loading docks
- **Military installations**: Barricades, watchtowers, Humvees, fenced perimeters

All geometry uses triplanar-mapped textures generated by the ComfyUI art pipeline. The NPR post-processing makes even simple box buildings look like watercolor illustrations.

### The Comedy

The humor comes from the collision of a wobbly alien blob with mundane human infrastructure:

- A translucent slime oozing into the driver's seat of a sedan, particles flooding the dashboard
- Floppy ragdoll humans tumbling over the hood as the slime-powered car plows through a crosswalk
- Fire hydrants spraying watercolor streams when clipped by a bumper
- Police cars with wailing sirens sliding sideways into mailboxes
- A gelatinous blob carefully browsing a junkyard for transmission upgrades

## 2. Core Loop: 30 Seconds of Fun

Following Jaime Griesemer's framework from Bungie -- every moment-to-moment action should be satisfying, and no 30-second stretch should repeat.

### The 3-Second Loop (Micro)

> Swerve to dodge a police cruiser, fire the elemental turret at two soldiers, grab scrap from the wreckage.

- **Input**: Steering (WASD/mouse) + weapon fire (left click) + scrap pickup (auto)
- **Feedback**: Tire screech SFX, particle projectile impact, scrap magnet pull animation
- **Feel**: Responsive, weighty, immediate consequence

### The 30-Second Loop (Combat Encounter)

> Drive into a human blockade. Ram through the barricade (physics debris). Deploy fire turret on clustered soldiers. Switch to spell launcher for the riot shield guy. Humans ragdoll in every direction. Scavenge dropped blueprints and scrap from the carnage. Vehicle takes scratches -- cosmetic damage appears on fender.

**Why it doesn't repeat**: The variables that change every 30 seconds:
- **Environment geometry**: Different street layouts, cover positions, elevation
- **Weapon loadout**: Player's turret type, spell element, utility slots
- **Vehicle state**: Damage level affects handling, motivates tactical caution
- **Enemy composition**: Mix of civilians (flee), police (pursue), soldiers (tactical), shields (block)
- **Scrap economy**: What dropped, what's needed for the next upgrade

### The 3-Minute Loop (Mission Scale)

> Explore a new neighborhood. Spot a garage in a cul-de-sac -- it has off-road tire blueprints. Equip the new tires. Notice the military checkpoint at the end of the block. Plan approach: take the alley to flank, use nitro boost to ram the barricade, then turret the watchtower. Assault. Loot the depot for an engine upgrade. Move to next district.

**Why it doesn't repeat**: Each district has a different layout, different enemy density, different loot tables, and different environmental hazards (narrow alleys vs. open intersections vs. elevated highway overpasses).

## 3. The Slime Scavenger Fantasy

### The Power Arc

The game's core emotional arc is **vulnerability to dominance**:

1. **Vulnerable**: On foot as a wobbly blob, you are slow, squishy, and pathetic. Humans with pistols are a genuine threat. You dodge, you hide, you flee.

2. **Empowered**: Inside a vehicle, your particles power the engine. Your elemental nature charges the weapons. You are a force of nature in a stolen sedan.

3. **Stripped**: Vehicle destroyed (GTA-style explosion). You pop out as the vulnerable blob again. Scramble for cover. Find another ride. The cycle renews.

### On-Foot State

The existing `BlobPlayerController` handles this. The slime is a `ParticleLifeCreature` with:
- **Vitality particles** (red): Health pool, depletes when shot
- **Essence particles** (white): Mana for the wand, enables basic combat
- **Shell particles** (grey): Minimal armor, can't take sustained fire
- **Elemental particles**: Fire/water/earth/air/shadow/light -- determine vehicle weapon type

On foot, the slime retains the wand (Chromatic Wand system from the story bible). The wand provides ranged defense but low DPS. The slime is incentivized to find a vehicle as quickly as possible.

Movement on foot uses the existing momentum-based controller: acceleration/deceleration physics, sliding (Shift), variable jump height (hold Space). The slime bounces, squishes, and wobbles via `BubblePhysics`.

### In-Vehicle State

When the slime enters a vehicle:
- Particle stream animation shows essence flowing from blob into the vehicle chassis
- **Vitality particles become vehicle HP** (scaled by a multiplier based on vehicle class)
- **Essence particles become weapon energy** (ammo/cooldown resource)
- **Shell particles add to vehicle armor rating**
- **Elemental particles determine weapon damage type and visual**
- Movement switches from `BlobPlayerController` to `JeepController` (existing vehicle system)

### The Scavenger Loop

```
ON FOOT (weak)
  |
  v
FIND VEHICLE (exploration reward)
  |
  v
ENTER VEHICLE (empowerment moment, 1.8s particle transfer animation)
  |
  v
COMBAT + SCAVENGE (core gameplay, 30-second loops)
  |
  v
VEHICLE DESTROYED (tension spike, GTA explosion)
  |
  v
EJECT AS BLOB (vulnerability returns)
  |
  v
SCRAMBLE FOR NEXT VEHICLE (survival pressure)
  |
  v
(repeat)
```

## 4. Vehicle-Spell Integration

### Particle-to-Vehicle Mapping

The slime's internal particle ecosystem directly powers the vehicle. This creates a seamless bridge between the blob creature system and vehicle combat.

| Particle Type | On-Foot Role | In-Vehicle Role | Mapping |
|---------------|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| **Vitality** (red) | Health | Vehicle HP | 1 vitality particle = 5 vehicle HP |
| **Essence** (white) | Mana (wand fuel) | Weapon energy pool | 1 essence = 1 weapon charge |
| **Shell** (grey) | Armor | Vehicle armor rating | Absorbs damage before HP |
| **Elemental** (varied) | Spell type | Weapon damage type | Fire slime = fire turret |

### Elemental Weapon Types

The slime's dominant element determines the vehicle weapon's visual and effect:

| Element | Turret Visual | Projectile | On-Hit Effect |
|---------|--------------|------------|---------------|
| **Fire** (crimson) | Red-hot barrel, ember particles | Fireball orb | Burn DOT (5 dmg/sec, 3s) |
| **Water** (azure) | Frost-coated nozzle, ice crystals | Ice shard | Slow (40% for 2s) |
| **Earth** (verdant) | Vine-wrapped barrel, leaf particles | Rock chunk | Knockback (stagger) |
| **Air** (ivory/white) | Crackling, static discharge | Lightning bolt | Pierce (hits 2 targets) |
| **Shadow** (violet) | Dark mist, void particles | Void orb | Drain (steal 10% as HP) |
| **Light** (golden) | Radiant glow, sun particles | Laser beam | Reveal + ignore armor |

### Dual-Element Combos

Players with multiple elemental cores can combine weapon effects:

| Combo | Effect | Visual |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Fire + Water | Steam Burst: AOE blind + damage | White steam cloud explosion |
| Fire + Earth | Magma Ball: Heavy DOT + slow | Glowing orange boulder |
| Water + Air | Cryo Lance: Freeze + pierce | Blue-white crystalline bolt |
| Earth + Air | Sandstorm: AOE slow + damage | Brown particle vortex |
| Shadow + Light | Twilight Cannon: Bonus damage to all types | Purple-gold swirl |
| Fire + Light | Solar Flare: Massive burst, self-damage | Blinding golden explosion |

## 5. Component Taxonomy

Vehicles are built from modular components installed in socket slots. Each component has weight, durability, elemental affinity, and physics effects.

### Component Categories

| Category | Socket Locations | Physics Effect | Examples |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|----------|
| **Propulsion** | 4 wheel slots | Speed, traction, terrain handling | Off-road tires, hover pads, tank treads, spiked wheels |
| **Engine** | 1 core slot | Max speed, acceleration, fuel efficiency | Turbo engine, elemental reactor, diesel block, hybrid core |
| **Armor** | 4 panel slots (front/back/left/right) | Weight, damage resistance, rollover threshold | Steel plates, crystal shield, spike armor, ceramic composite |
| **Weapon** | 4 mount slots (roof/front/left/right) | Weight distribution, ammo type, fire rate | Turret, spell launcher, mine dropper, ram blade |
| **Utility** | 2 slots | Various special effects | Scrap magnet, repair drone, nitro boost, smoke screen |

### Socket Layout

```
           [ROOF WEAPON]
          _______________
         /               \
[L ARMOR]|  [ENGINE]     |[R ARMOR]
[L WEAPON]|              |[R WEAPON]
         |   [UTIL 1]    |
         |   [UTIL 2]    |
[F ARMOR]\_______________/[B ARMOR]
     [FL WHEEL][FR WHEEL]
     [BL WHEEL][BR WHEEL]
           [FRONT WEAPON]
```

### Weight Budget System

Every component has a weight value. Total weight affects:
- **Acceleration**: Heavier = slower to reach top speed
- **Top speed**: Diminishing returns above weight threshold
- **Handling**: Heavier = wider turning radius
- **Rollover**: Heavier = more stable but harder to recover
- **Suspension**: Determines ground clearance and terrain response

Base vehicle chassis weight: **1000 kg**
Weight budget: **+2000 kg** additional components
Performance curve: Linear degradation from 0 to 1500 kg, steep degradation from 1500 to 2000 kg

### Example Builds

**Glass Cannon**
- Engine: Turbo Engine (+50% speed, 200 kg)
- Armor: None (0 kg)
- Weapons: 2x Spell Launchers (300 kg each)
- Propulsion: Racing tires (+20% traction, 100 kg total)
- Utility: Nitro Boost, Scrap Magnet
- Total added: 900 kg -- fast, lethal, fragile

**Rolling Fortress**
- Engine: Diesel Block (moderate speed, 400 kg)
- Armor: 4x Steel Plates (500 kg each = 2000 kg)
- Weapons: 1x Roof Turret (250 kg)
- Propulsion: Tank Treads (350 kg total)
- Utility: Repair Drone, Smoke Screen
- Total added: 3000 kg (over budget) -- slow, tanky, needs weight reduction

**Hit-and-Run**
- Engine: Hybrid Core (good acceleration, 250 kg)
- Armor: Front Crystal Shield only (300 kg)
- Weapons: Front Ram Blade (150 kg), Mine Dropper (200 kg)
- Propulsion: Off-road Tires (150 kg total)
- Utility: Nitro Boost, Smoke Screen
- Total added: 1050 kg -- balanced, mobile, tactical

## 6. Scavenging System

### How Components Are Found

Components enter the player's inventory through three channels:

**1. Enemy Drops (Combat Rewards)**
- Defeated humans drop **component blueprints** (25% chance) and **scrap materials** (100% chance)
- Blueprint rarity scales with enemy difficulty:
  - Civilians: Common only
  - Police: Common, 10% Uncommon
  - Soldiers: Common-Uncommon, 5% Rare
  - Riot Shields: Uncommon-Rare
  - Snipers: Rare, 10% Epic
  - Commanders: Rare-Epic guaranteed, 5% Legendary

**2. World Exploration (Discovery Rewards)**
- **Garages**: Contain 1-3 components, often propulsion or engine parts
- **Junkyards**: Large caches of Common-Uncommon components, high scrap density
- **Military depots**: Weapon blueprints, Rare+ armor, guarded by soldiers
- **Abandoned vehicles**: Can be stripped for 1-2 components matching the vehicle type
- **Hidden caches**: Buried in alleys, rooftops, basement entrances -- reward exploration

**3. Crafting (Combination)**
- Combine scrap + blueprint at any safe zone
- Scrap types: Metal (armor), Circuitry (weapons), Fuel (engine), Rubber (propulsion)
- Higher rarity requires more scrap and rarer materials

### Rarity Tiers

| Tier | Color | Drop Weight | Stat Range | Durability | Elemental Affinity |
|------|-------|-------------|------------|------------|--------------------|
| **Common** | White | 60% | Base stats | 500 HP | None |
| **Uncommon** | Green | 25% | +10-20% | 750 HP | Weak affinity (1 element) |
| **Rare** | Blue | 10% | +25-40% | 1000 HP | Moderate affinity |
| **Epic** | Purple | 4% | +45-60% | 1500 HP | Strong affinity (2 elements) |
| **Legendary** | Orange | 1% | +65-100% | 2500 HP | Perfect affinity + unique passive |

### Component Durability

Components degrade during combat and environmental wear:
- **Armor**: Loses durability when hit (1:1 with absorbed damage)
- **Weapons**: Lose durability per shot fired (1 per shot)
- **Propulsion**: Loses durability from terrain impacts and jumps (varies)
- **Engine**: Degrades slowly over time (1/minute of operation)
- **Utility**: Loses durability per activation

When durability reaches 0, the component is **destroyed** and must be replaced. This creates churn in the economy and incentivizes scavenging.

### Elemental Affinity

Components with elemental affinity gain bonuses when used by a matching-element slime:
- **Matching element**: +25% effectiveness, -15% weight
- **Opposing element**: -25% effectiveness, +15% weight
- **Neutral**: No modifier

Example: A Fire-affinity Turbo Engine in a Fire slime's vehicle gets +25% speed bonus and weighs 15% less.

## 7. Human Enemy Types

All human enemies use a TABS-style ragdoll system: procedural wobbly bodies that flop, tumble, and fly satisfyingly on impact. No mocap animations -- physics drives all motion.

### Enemy Roster

| Type | Role | Move Speed | HP | Armor | Behavior | Visual Description |
|------|------|-----------|-----|-------|----------|-------------------|
| **Civilian** | Panicked runner | 0.15 | 30 | 0 | Flee from player, run in random directions, trip and fall | Casual clothing (T-shirt, jeans). Flailing arms. Terrified expression. |
| **Police** | Basic combat | 0.12 | 80 | 20 | Pursue player in patrol cars, exit and shoot pistol, call for backup | Blue uniform, cap. Pistol in hand. Radio on shoulder. |
| **Soldier** | Standard combat | 0.10 | 120 | 40 | Tactical approach in squads of 3-4, take cover, fire rifles in bursts | Green camo fatigues, helmet. Rifle. Body armor vest. |
| **Riot Shield** | Tank / blocker | 0.06 | 200 | 100 | Advance in formation, block projectiles from front, push player vehicle back | Heavy armor suit, riot shield (large plane geometry). Visor helmet. |
| **Sniper** | Ranged / glass cannon | 0.08 | 60 | 10 | Position on rooftops/elevated positions, high single-shot damage, relocate after 3 shots | Ghillie suit (mesh with displaced vertices). Scoped rifle. Prone position. |
| **Commander** | Boss / leader | 0.10 | 500 | 80 | Call reinforcement waves, deploy barricades, enter armored Humvee. 3 combat phases. | Decorated uniform with medals (colored planes). Command radio. Beret. |

### Behavior Details

**Civilians**
- Spawn in groups of 5-10 in residential/commercial areas
- Run away from player at random angles when alerted
- Trip and ragdoll when hit by vehicle (low threshold: 5 units/sec)
- Drop: Small scrap (5-10 units), no blueprints
- Alerting civilians triggers a 30-second timer before police arrive

**Police**
- Spawn in patrol cars (pairs), respond to civilian alerts
- Phase 1: Chase player in patrol car, try to ram/block
- Phase 2: Exit vehicle, draw pistol, shoot from cover
- Pistol: 10 damage per shot, 1.5s fire rate, 15m range
- Drop: Metal scrap (15), 10% Uncommon blueprint

**Soldiers**
- Spawn at military checkpoints and in response to high alert
- Move in squads of 3-4, use cover (walls, cars, barricades)
- Rifle: 15 damage per shot, 0.8s fire rate, 30m range, burst of 3
- Throw grenades at stationary targets (40 damage AOE, 3m radius)
- Drop: Circuitry scrap (20), Metal scrap (10), 15% blueprint (Common-Rare)

**Riot Shield**
- Spawn at fortified positions, military bases
- Shield blocks all frontal damage (must flank or use AOE)
- Shield bash: 30 damage, pushes vehicle 2m backward
- Slow but relentless advance, cannot be knocked down by sub-20 speed impacts
- Drop: Heavy armor blueprint guaranteed (Common-Rare), Metal scrap (30)

**Sniper**
- Spawn on rooftops, highway overpasses, watchtowers
- Sniper shot: 50 damage, ignores 50% armor, 60m range
- Visible laser sight (red line) warns player 1.5s before shot
- Relocates to new position after 3 shots or if player gets within 10m
- Drop: Weapon blueprint (Uncommon-Epic), Circuitry scrap (25)

**Commander (Boss)**
- Spawns at end of each district as the final encounter
- **Phase 1** (100-60% HP): On foot, calls waves of soldiers every 20s. Pistol + radio.
- **Phase 2** (60-30% HP): Enters armored Humvee. Vehicle must be destroyed first (600 HP). Mounted gun turret (25 damage, 0.5s rate).
- **Phase 3** (30-0% HP): Humvee destroyed, exits with heavy weapon (rocket launcher). 60 damage rockets, 3s cooldown, tracking.
- Drop: Guaranteed Epic+ blueprint, 50 Metal scrap, 30 Circuitry scrap, 20 Fuel

### Alert System

Human enemy presence scales with player aggression:

| Alert Level | Trigger | Response | Timer |
|-------------|---------|----------|-------|
| **0 - Calm** | Default | Civilians only | -- |
| **1 - Suspicious** | Civilian spots slime | Police dispatched (2 cars) | 30s to level 2 |
| **2 - Alert** | Police engaged | More police + first soldiers | 60s to level 3 |
| **3 - Hostile** | Soldiers engaged | Full military response (soldiers + snipers) | 120s to level 4 |
| **4 - Siege** | Sustained combat | Riot shields + Commander boss | Persists until Commander defeated |
| **5 - Lockdown** | Commander defeated | Alert resets to 0 after 30s cooldown | 30s decay |

Alert decays by 1 level every 45 seconds if the player breaks line of sight and stops engaging.

## 8. GTA-Style Vehicle Damage

### Damage States

Vehicle damage is continuous (0-100%) but visual effects trigger at discrete thresholds:

| State | HP Range | Visual Effects | Physics Penalties |
|-------|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **PRISTINE** | 100-80% | Clean chassis, no marks | None |
| **SCRATCHED** | 80-60% | Light scratches (vertex displacement on panels), dirty texture blend | None |
| **DENTED** | 60-40% | Visible dents (mesh deformation), cracked windshield (opacity change), one headlight out | -5% top speed |
| **DAMAGED** | 40-20% | Major body damage, panels hanging (detached geometry), sparks from undercarriage (particle emitter), both headlights out | -15% top speed, -10% handling |
| **CRITICAL** | 20-10% | Severe deformation, trailing smoke (dark particle stream), engine sputtering (intermittent speed drops) | -30% top speed, -25% handling, random stalls (0.5s pause every 5-10s) |
| **BURNING** | 10-0% | Active fire (flame particles from hood), black smoke column, interior glow | -50% speed, -40% handling, continuous HP drain (2%/sec) |
| **DESTROYED** | 0% | Explosion (particle burst + flash + shockwave), chassis becomes static debris, slime ejected | Vehicle unusable. 3s eject animation. |

### Damage Sources

| Source | Damage | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Pistol shot | 10 | Reduced by armor |
| Rifle burst (3 shots) | 45 | Reduced by armor |
| Grenade | 40 | AOE, ignores 30% armor |
| Sniper shot | 50 | Ignores 50% armor |
| Rocket | 60 | AOE, ignores 60% armor |
| Vehicle ram (police car) | 20-50 | Scales with relative speed |
| Environmental collision | 5-30 | Scales with speed at impact |
| Fall damage | 10-80 | Scales with height |
| Barricade ram | 15 | Per barricade piece |

### Damage Visualization Implementation

Each damage state modifies the vehicle mesh and adds particle effects:

```
PRISTINE:  Original mesh, clean materials
SCRATCHED: Vertex noise displacement on armor panels (amplitude 0.02)
DENTED:    Vertex noise displacement increased (amplitude 0.08), mesh deformation toward center
DAMAGED:   Detach panel sub-meshes (random 1-2 panels become loose physics objects)
           Spark emitter at detachment points
CRITICAL:  Smoke particle system from engine (dark grey, rises)
           Random material flicker (emissive flash simulating electrical shorts)
BURNING:   Fire particle system (orange-red, from hood/engine area)
           Smoke increases to black column
           Chassis material shifts to charred dark
DESTROYED: Explosion: sphere of particles outward + white flash + ground decal
           Chassis mesh stays as static burnt-out wreck (environmental obstacle)
```

### Repair Mechanics

- **Repair Drone** (utility component): Slowly repairs 2% HP per second while not taking damage
- **Repair Stations**: Found at garages and safe zones, full repair over 5 seconds
- **Component Swap**: Replacing a destroyed armor panel restores its damage reduction
- **No self-repair**: Without repair components/stations, damage is permanent until a new vehicle

## 9. Enter/Exit Mechanic

### State Machine

```
                    E key (near vehicle)
ON_FOOT ────────────────────────────> ENTERING
  ^                                      |
  |                                      | (1.8s animation)
  |                                      v
  |      E key                        DRIVING
  |  <──────────────────────────────     |
  |          (1.4s animation)            |
EXITING <────────────────────────────────+
  ^          E key (voluntary)
  |
  +──── EJECTED (vehicle destroyed, 0.6s forced exit)
```

### States

**ON_FOOT**
- Player uses `BlobPlayerController` (momentum movement, wand combat)
- `ParticleLifeCreature` is visible and active
- Can interact with world objects, scavenge ground items
- Approaching a drivable vehicle within 3m shows "Press E to enter" prompt

**ENTERING** (1.8 seconds)
- Player blob moves toward vehicle driver position (lerp over 0.5s)
- Particle stream animation: particles flow from blob into vehicle chassis
  - Vitality (red) particles stream into the engine block area
  - Essence (white) particles flow into weapon mounts
  - Shell (grey) particles spread across armor panels
  - Elemental particles flood into weapon barrels
- Blob shrinks as particles transfer out (scale lerp from 1.0 to 0.1)
- At 1.8s: Blob disappears, vehicle activates
- Input is locked during this animation (player cannot cancel)

**DRIVING**
- Player uses `JeepController` (steering, throttle, brake)
- Vehicle weapons active (left click = fire, right click = alt fire)
- Camera switches to vehicle chase cam (pulled back, higher angle)
- HUD shows vehicle HP bar, weapon energy, component status
- `ParticleLifeCreature` is hidden (particles are "inside" the vehicle)

**EXITING** (1.4 seconds)
- Reverse of entering: particles stream out of vehicle back into forming blob
- Blob materializes at driver-side door position
- Vehicle becomes inert (parked, still collidable)
- At 1.4s: Full blob restored, `BlobPlayerController` active
- Vehicle remains in world and can be re-entered

**EJECTED** (0.6 seconds, forced)
- Vehicle HP reaches 0 -- explosion triggers
- Blob is forcefully launched upward and backward (physics impulse)
- Particles scatter chaotically, then reconverge into blob shape
- Brief invulnerability window (1.5s) to prevent instant death from explosion damage
- Blob spawns at 60% HP (some vitality particles were lost in the explosion)

### Vehicle Discovery

Drivable vehicles are placed in the world as interactive objects:

| Vehicle Type | Location | Base HP | Speed | Handling | Weapon Slots |
|--------------|----------|---------|-------|----------|--------------|
| **Sedan** | Suburban streets | 400 | Medium | Good | 2 (roof, front) |
| **Pickup Truck** | Garages, rural | 600 | Medium | Moderate | 3 (roof, bed x2) |
| **Sports Car** | Downtown | 300 | Fast | Excellent | 1 (front) |
| **SUV** | Parking lots | 500 | Medium | Good | 3 (roof, left, right) |
| **Humvee** | Military bases | 800 | Slow | Poor | 4 (roof, front, left, right) |
| **Semi Truck** | Industrial zone | 1000 | Very Slow | Terrible | 4 (all slots) + ram damage bonus |

All vehicles use the existing `JeepPhysics` bicycle model with parameters adjusted per type (wheelbase, steer speed, max speed, mass).

## 10. Progression

### Act Structure

The game progresses through districts of increasing difficulty, each culminating in a Commander boss fight.

**Act 1: Awakening** (Tutorial District)

1. Player spawns as a slime blob in a suburban parking lot
2. Brief on-foot section: learn movement, wand basics, kill 2-3 fleeing civilians
3. Discover first vehicle: an abandoned sedan in the parking lot (keys in ignition)
4. Enter vehicle (first enter animation plays with UI tutorial callouts)
5. Drive tutorial: navigate a few blocks, run over ragdoll civilians
6. First police encounter: 1 patrol car, learn vehicle combat basics
7. Find a garage: first component swap (better tires)
8. District boss: Police Commander with 2 patrol car escorts

**Act 2: Escalation** (Downtown District)

1. Streets get denser, more cover for soldiers
2. Military checkpoints appear at intersections
3. Junkyards contain Uncommon+ components
4. Introduce soldiers, grenades, cover mechanics
5. Introduce vehicle variety (pickup trucks, sports cars in dealer lots)
6. District boss: Military Commander with soldiers + sniper support

**Act 3: Fortification** (Industrial District)

1. Narrow warehouse corridors force close combat
2. Riot shields block direct approaches, require flanking
3. Heavy weapon components available (spell launchers, mine droppers)
4. Introduce elemental combo weapons (dual-element turrets)
5. Military depot has Epic components behind heavy guard
6. District boss: Armored Commander with Humvee + riot shield escort

**Act 4: Siege** (Military Base)

1. Full military installation with fences, watchtowers, barricades
2. All enemy types present simultaneously
3. Legendary component caches in the armory
4. Humvees available for the player to hijack
5. Final boss: Base Commander with 3 phases, calls all enemy types

**Act 5: Open World** (Post-Game)

1. All districts accessible with refreshed enemy spawns
2. Increased rarity drops across the board
3. Challenge modes: timed runs, no-vehicle runs, specific loadout challenges
4. Legendary-only blueprint drops from special world events

### Slime Growth

The Particle Life creature grows through the game:

| Progression | Particle Count | Bubble Radius | New Ability |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|-------------|
| Start | 100 particles | 0.5m | Wand only |
| Act 1 complete | 150 particles | 0.6m | Unlock first element core |
| Act 2 complete | 200 particles | 0.7m | Unlock dual-element combos |
| Act 3 complete | 300 particles | 0.8m | Unlock 4 weapon mount slots |
| Act 4 complete | 400 particles | 1.0m | Unlock legendary slot |

Growth is visible -- the player's blob gets bigger, denser, more colorful as they progress. A late-game slime entering a vehicle produces a much more dramatic particle stream than an early-game one.

## 11. Technical Notes

### Geometry

All 3D geometry is procedural, built from Three.js primitives:
- **Buildings**: `BoxGeometry` with triplanar-mapped textures
- **Vehicles**: Composed `BoxGeometry` + `CylinderGeometry` (wheels) + `PlaneGeometry` (windshield)
- **Humans**: TABS-style wobblers -- `CapsuleGeometry` torso, `SphereGeometry` head, `CylinderGeometry` limbs, connected by spring joints for ragdoll
- **Props**: Fire hydrants (`CylinderGeometry`), lamp posts (`CylinderGeometry` + `SphereGeometry`), barricades (`BoxGeometry` stacks)
- **Terrain**: Chunked heightmap mesh, 64x64 vertices per chunk, Perlin noise displacement

### Textures

AI-generated 2D textures via the existing ComfyUI art pipeline:
- Watercolor-style diffuse maps for buildings, roads, terrain
- Triplanar projection eliminates UV mapping for box geometry
- NPR pipeline applies Kuwahara + edge + paper on top, so textures can be simple

### Rendering Pipeline

The existing NPR pipeline is preserved:
1. **RenderPass**: Base 3D scene with all geometry
2. **KuwaharaPass**: Painterly brush strokes (radius 3-4)
3. **EdgeDarkeningPass**: Ink outlines on geometry edges
4. **UnrealBloomPass**: Glow on elemental effects, explosions, weapon fire
5. **PaperTexturePass**: Grain + vignette + warm color grading

The color transfer system (Lab-space Reinhard) keeps the warm illustrated palette. Per the existing tuning notes, color transfer strength stays at 0.20 to preserve sky blue while warming terrain.

## Appendix C: Sound Design

| Event | Sound Type | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Vehicle engine | Procedural oscillator | Pitch scales with speed, rumble with damage state |
| Turret fire | Procedural burst | Element determines timbre (fire=crackle, ice=shatter, etc.) |
| Human screams | Short procedural tones | Pitch-shifted, Doppler effect as they ragdoll past |
| Vehicle collision | Noise burst + low thud | Amplitude scales with impact speed |
| Enter/exit | Particle stream swoosh | Rising pitch on enter, falling on exit |
| Explosion | Layered noise + sine decay | Low rumble + high crackle, reverb tail |
| Scrap pickup | Metallic chime | Pitch scales with scrap value |
| Alert level change | Siren/horn stinger | Gets more intense with higher alert |

All sounds use the existing `Web Audio API` procedural system -- no audio file assets needed.
