# Chromatic Resonance - The Bloom: Story Bible

**Status**: Active Design Document
**Last Updated**: 2026-02-09
**Setting**: Far North Queensland, Australia

---

## Executive Summary

Chromatic Resonance takes place in the remote outback and tropical lowlands of Far North Queensland. The player is a self-organizing particle blob that escaped a classified CSIRO research facility built into the limestone cave systems near Chillagoe. The game follows the blob's desperate journey east — from the semi-arid karst country, across the volcanic tablelands, and down to the cyclone-scarred tropical coast — pursued by ADF kill teams who classified it as a containment breach before anyone realised it was alive.

**Tone**: Australian Gothic meets survival horror. The blob isn't evil — it's pathetic and resourceful. Players discover the backstory through environmental lore (drilling logs, ADF radio intercepts, sun-bleached newspaper clippings), not cutscenes.

## Part 1: World Lore

### The CSIRO Chillagoe Deep-Core Facility

A classified CSIRO particle physics facility built into the limestone cave systems near **Chillagoe**, a remote town 215km west of Cairns. The karst geology — hundreds of ancient limestone caves formed over millions of years — provided natural containment chambers deep underground. Scientists experimenting with exotic matter containment in the cave network accidentally created a self-organizing particle field: matter that *wants* to be alive.

The experiment was sealed. Internal review boards recommended full shutdown, but funding continued under a new classification level. The particle field didn't die. Over years it absorbed waste, chemicals, scrap metal from the facility — slowly forming a crude awareness.

### The Bloom

One day, it pulled itself out of the containment pool.

A wobbling, translucent blob the size of a basketball. Almost no particles. Barely able to move. It could feel other particles nearby — in machines, in fuel, in the walls. It reached out and *absorbed*.

The facility's automated systems registered a containment breach. By the time human operators responded, the blob had consumed half a corridor of equipment and grown to the size of a car.

### The ADF Response

The Australian Defence Force classified it as ECHO-BLACK — a biological containment breach with potential WMD implications. Kill orders were issued before anyone realised it was alive. The blob doesn't understand this. It just knows everything with legs wants it dead.

### The Journey

The blob escapes the underground facility into the blinding outback sun. It drives east, away from the facility, absorbing vehicles, scrap metal, and fuel along the way. Its crude intelligence grows with every absorption. It doesn't have goals in the human sense — it moves toward complexity, toward more particles, toward survival.

The route takes it along real Far North Queensland roads: Burke Developmental Road to Kennedy Highway, climbing through the volcanic tablelands, then plunging down the infamous Gillies Highway (263 hairpin curves, dropping 800 metres in 19 kilometres) to the tropical coastal lowlands.

## Part 2: Particle Mechanics

### Core Particle Types

The blob's body is a self-organizing particle ecosystem. Four core types define its capabilities:

| Core | Colour | Function | Source |
|------|--------|----------|--------|
| **Vitality** | Crimson | Health, regeneration, structural integrity | Combat kills |
| **Shell** | Azure | Armour, damage reduction, membrane strength | Absorbing metal/equipment |
| **Spark** | Gold | Energy, speed, attack power | Electrical/fuel sources |
| **Essence** | Violet | Mana, special abilities, particle projection | Anomalous matter |

### Particle Behaviour

Particles within the blob follow Particle Life rules — attractive and repulsive forces between types create emergent behaviour:

- **High Vitality**: Dense, resilient core. Hard to kill but slow.
- **High Shell**: Thick membrane. Deflects projectiles but reduces mobility.
- **High Spark**: Energetic, fast-moving particles. High damage output but fragile.
- **High Essence**: Ethereal, glowing particles. Enables special abilities but burns through resources.

The player doesn't directly control particle balance — it emerges from what they absorb, what enemies they fight, and what cores they upgrade between zones.

### Bubble Physics

The blob's outer membrane deforms dynamically:
- **Compression** on impact (squash and stretch)
- **Wobble** during movement (momentum-based deformation)
- **Ground sag** when stationary (settling physics)
- **Turn bulge** during direction changes (inertia visualisation)

## Part 3: Creature Design

### The Player Blob

A translucent sphere containing swirling particles. At game start, it's small and dim — barely visible particles in a fragile membrane. As it absorbs more matter, it grows in size, density, and luminosity. The visual progression tells the story: from pathetic escapee to formidable entity.

### Wobbler Enemies

Enemies are TABS-style (Totally Accurate Battle Simulator) humanoid wobblers — ragdoll-like bipeds with exaggerated physics. Their limbs flail, they stumble, they topple. They're simultaneously threatening and absurd.

## Part 4: Zones

### Zone 0 — Chillagoe Research Site

**Real Inspiration**: Chillagoe mines, Irvinebank smelter ruins, limestone caves

**Environment**: Semi-arid outback karst country. Jagged limestone spires rise from red dirt like broken teeth. Abandoned smelter chimneys from the old tin/copper mining era tower over collapsed sheds. The research facility entrance is a reinforced concrete structure built into the base of a limestone ridge. Scattered mine shafts, containment tanks leaking viscous fluid, chain-link fencing torn open.

**Atmosphere**: Blinding sun, bone-dry heat, red dust on everything. The only sounds are wind through limestone crevices and distant generator hum from the facility. Everything bleached by decades of 40-degree summers.

**Gameplay**: The blob's first outdoor experience. Enemies are facility security — limited numbers, moderate equipment. The facility's automated defences (turrets, blast doors) are offline. Scrap and vehicle parts scattered among mining equipment.

### Zone 1 — Herberton Scrapyard

**Real Inspiration**: Herberton Historic Village, Mt Mulligan ghost town, Ravenshoe (highest town in QLD)

**Environment**: Volcanic tablelands at 920m elevation. Rolling hills of red volcanic soil, corrugated iron sheds, and rusted mining equipment from the tin rush era. Crater lakes sit in old volcanic vents. Old narrow-gauge rail lines run through the settlement. Mist hangs in the valleys. Temperature has dropped noticeably from the lowlands.

**Atmosphere**: Ghost town energy. Once-prosperous mining settlements now populated by a handful of holdouts, prospectors, and people who don't want to be found. Eucalypt woodland, grass trees, granite outcrops. The kind of place where you can drive for an hour without seeing another person.

**Gameplay**: Open terrain with scattered cover. Enemies are local — militia, dogs, ute drivers. More mobile, less coordinated than facility security. The scrapyard itself is a treasure trove of vehicle parts.

### Zone 2 — Innisfail

**Real Inspiration**: Innisfail Art Deco town centre, Johnstone River, sugar cane fields

**Environment**: Tropical coastal lowland. The town features Queensland's finest collection of Art Deco architecture — pastel facades with stepped parapets, now damaged by decades of cyclones. Sugar cane fields surround the town: 4-metre-tall corridors of dense green, burning at harvest time with ash falling like black snow. The Johnstone River runs through town, mangrove-lined, with crocodile warning signs at every access point.

**Atmosphere**: Humid, oppressive, tropical. Cyclone-damaged streetscapes. ADF has established a blockade at the river bridge. The blob can feel the ocean somewhere beyond the cane fields — the terminus of its journey. Burning cane throws an orange glow across the evening sky.

**Gameplay**: Tightest, most dangerous zone. Police, ADF soldiers, and snipers. Urban combat through narrow streets and cane field corridors. The ADF blockade is the final obstacle.

## Part 5: NPCs

NPCs are recontextualised for the modern Earth setting:

### The Keeper (Stationary Guide)
An abandoned lab terminal that still has partial AI functionality. Speaks in fragmented research logs. Provides hints about facility layout, enemy patterns, and the blob's nature. Found in safe areas near zone entrances.

### The Wanderer (Patrol NPC)
A former CSIRO researcher who went into hiding when the ADF arrived. Moves between locations. Speaks in paranoid whispers. Provides context about what the facility was doing and why the ADF response is so extreme.

### The Fragment (Follower)
A smaller particle anomaly — a piece of the original containment field that separated during the breach. Follows the player. Communicates in broken impressions rather than words. Unstable, flickering, pathetic.

## Part 6: Environmental Storytelling

All backstory is delivered through discoverable lore pickups — glowing documents scattered throughout zones. No cutscenes, no exposition dumps. Players piece together the story from fragments.

### Lore Types

**Drilling Logs** (Zone 0): Day-by-day accounts of the facility's descent into disaster. Start clinical, become increasingly alarmed.

**ADF Radio Intercepts** (All zones): Military communications revealing the ADF's response escalation. Start with "containment advisory" and end with "lethal force authorised against all anomalous biological entities."

**Sun-Bleached Newspaper Clippings** (Zones 1-2): Local newspaper articles from the Innisfail Advocate and Cairns Post. Civilian perspective on the crisis — rumours, evacuations, cane harvest cancellations.

**Personal Items** (All zones): Cracked ID badges, voicemail transcripts, scrawled notes. Human details that make the facility staff real — birthday cards found in abandoned offices, photos taped to monitors.

**Spray Paint / Graffiti** (Zone 2): Civilian responses. "THE BLOB IS ALIVE — IT JUST WANTS TO LEAVE." Evidence that some humans recognise the blob isn't malicious.

## Part 7: Discovery Mechanics

### Noita-Style Secrets

The game rewards experimentation and exploration:

- **Augment Combos**: Certain augment combinations produce unexpected synergies. Not documented in-game — players discover through experimentation.
- **Core Fusion**: Maxing multiple core types simultaneously triggers fusion events with unique visual effects.
- **Hidden Areas**: Each zone has off-path areas with concentrated lore pickups. Cave systems in Zone 0, abandoned mine shafts in Zone 1, mangrove channels in Zone 2.
- **Vehicle Secrets**: Certain scrap combinations produce vehicle modifications not listed in the workshop UI.

### Meta-Progression

Core Fragments persist across runs in localStorage. Each fragment provides a small permanent bonus to the blob's starting stats, making subsequent runs slightly easier while maintaining challenge through zone difficulty scaling.

## Part 8: FNQ Atmosphere — Australian Gothic

Far North Queensland provides a unique atmosphere that no other setting offers:

### Karst Country (Zone 0)
- Limestone spires rising from red dirt like broken bones
- 600+ limestone caves, some unexplored
- Old smelter chimneys from the tin/copper rush (1880s-1920s)
- Termite mounds standing like sentinels along every road
- 300 sunny days per year, temperatures regularly above 40C
- Dust devils spinning across flat country

### Volcanic Tablelands (Zone 1)
- Crater lakes in extinct volcanic vents
- 920m elevation — cool, misty, completely different climate to the coast
- Ghost mining towns with populations under 50
- Old narrow-gauge rail lines disappearing into scrub
- Grass trees (blackboys) with fire-blackened trunks
- Granite boulders balanced impossibly on hillsides

### Tropical Coast (Zone 2)
- Art Deco architecture in pastel colours, cyclone-damaged
- Sugar cane fields — impenetrable green walls, 4m tall
- Cane burning at harvest: black ash falling like snow, orange sky
- Crocodile warning signs at every waterway
- Cassowary crossings on jungle roads
- Mangrove forests with pneumatophore roots like fingers
- Cyclone debris — roofing iron wrapped around power poles
- Humidity so thick you can taste it

## Part 9: Cut-Scene Transitions

Brief visual transitions play between zones, showing the blob's journey along real FNQ roads.

### Zone 0 to Zone 1: Burke Dev Rd to Kennedy Highway

Emerge from the underground facility into blinding outback sun. Red dirt road stretches to the horizon. Termite mounds stand like sentinels along both sides. The landscape gains elevation as the road climbs toward the Great Dividing Range. Eucalypt woodland thickens. Pass through Mareeba — 300 sunny days a year, mango orchards, coffee plantations. Begin climbing. Temperature drops. Mist appears in the valleys.

### Zone 1 to Zone 2: Gillies Highway

263 hairpin curves. 800-metre elevation drop in 19 kilometres. The blob blasts down the switchbacks, descending from cool misty tablelands into the wall-of-heat tropical lowlands. Vegetation shifts from eucalypt woodland to dense tropical rainforest. Giant strangler figs. Fan palms. Epiphytes dripping from every branch. Hit the coastal plain — flat, humid, towering sugar cane on both sides of the road. Cane fields burning in the evening light, columns of smoke, black ash falling like snow. Arrive at the cyclone-scarred town at dusk.

## Part 10: Future — Real Terrain Data

The game can use real-world elevation and satellite data for authentic FNQ terrain:

### Elevation Sources (Free)
- **ELVIS** (elevation.fsdf.org.au) — Australian government, best available resolution (30m standard, 1-5m LiDAR near populated areas)
- **ALOS AW3D30** via OpenTopography — clean 30m global data
- **SRTM 30m** — NASA, covers all of FNQ

### Satellite Imagery (Free)
- **Sentinel-2 Cloudless** (s2maps.eu) — 10m resolution, cloud-free composites, CC-BY licence
- **QImagery** (qimagery.information.qld.gov.au) — QLD government aerial photography

### Road/Infrastructure
- **OpenStreetMap** via Overpass API — roads, buildings, towns as GeoJSON

### Conversion Workflow (GDAL + Three.js)
1. Download DEM from ELVIS for zone area
2. Crop to bounding box: `gdal_translate -projwin <coords> input.tif cropped.tif`
3. Resize to 257x257: `gdal_translate -outsize 257 257 cropped.tif resized.tif`
4. Export as 16-bit PNG: `gdal_translate -scale <min> <max> 0 65535 -ot UInt16 -of PNG resized.tif heightmap.png`
5. Load in Three.js: replace `fbmNoise()` in `outdoor-level.js` with heightmap data
6. Apply 2-3x vertical exaggeration (real terrain is too flat at game scale)
7. Use satellite imagery as terrain texture

**Note**: Real terrain data is a future enhancement. For the alpha, the existing procedural terrain with zone-themed objects is sufficient.
